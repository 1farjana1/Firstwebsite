The Persian cat (Persian: گربه ایرانی‎, romanized: Gorbe Īrānī) is a long-haired breed of cat characterized by its round face and short muzzle. It is also known as the "Persian Longhair" in the English-speaking countries. In the Middle East region, they are widely known as "Shirazi cats" and in Iran they are known as "Shiraz cat". The first documented ancestors of the Persian were imported into Italy from Iran (historically known as Persia in the west) around 1620.

Persian Mastiff (Persian: سگ سرابی‎) is an ancient livestock guardian dog breed native to Sarab County in northern Iran.[2][3] Is a molosser-type dog with short coat. Its height varies about 71 to 89 cm at the withers, and its weight between 50 and 90 kg approximately.[4] They are friendly and affable with their owners, but very averse to strangers.[5][1] They are used as guard dogs and also to protect livestock against predators.[6] Among the common colors of the dog are the acceptable colors such as pale gray, gray, bronze and black, which are other characteristics of the dog.
